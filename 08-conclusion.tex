\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

This survey organized the rapidly evolving landscape of RL-based LLM
post-training through a compact taxonomy and identified three dominant
trends: a shift from subjective preferences toward \emph{verifiable}
reward signals that are cheaper, more reproducible, and less susceptible
to hacking; a convergence on \emph{critic-free, group-relative}
optimizers such as GRPO and RLOO that blend online exploration with the
simplicity of offline methods; and an expansion of the training scope
from single responses to full \emph{trajectories}, reviving classical
challenges of credit assignment and stateful memory for LLMs.

The open problems we surveyed, including reward hacking, credit
assignment, sample efficiency, scalable oversight, and multi-turn
evaluation, align with these trends and suggest that progress on
verifiable rewards, efficient group-relative optimization, and
trajectory-level training will be mutually reinforcing. As these
directions mature, RL is poised to remain the central mechanism in the
pursuit of reliable, general-purpose agentic intelligence.
